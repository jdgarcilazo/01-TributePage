Hugo Sánchez

The greatest Mexican Football Player

Here's a time line of Hugo Sánchez's life:

  • 1958 - Born in Mexico City, Mexico
  • 1976 - As a teenager, Hugo played for Mexico national team at the 1976 Summer Olympics. In this year he also started his professional career, playing for "Pumas de la UNAM" team in Mexican First Division Football League.
  • 1978 - In this year, he played his first World Cup with Mexican national team in Argentina. Unfortunately, Mexican team had a very bad performance.
  • 1979 - He played for the San Diego Sockers team in the North American Soccer League" during the summer & in the Mexican League during the fall, summer & spring.
  • 1981 - In his last year playing for "Pumas de la UNAM", he won with his team the Mexican League, a CONCACAF Champions League & a "Copa Interamericana". During his tenure playing in Mexico and US, he became a prolific striker & in this last year in Mexico, he was the top scorer with 26 goals.
  • 1982 - Atlético Madrid signed him. During the first 2 years, it took Hugo a while to find his feet in "La Liga". During these 2 years of struggle, he never gave up & even accepted a 50% reduction on his salary, just to continue playing in the best football leagues in Europe.
  • 1984 - In the 1984-1985 season, Hugo had his first big success in "La Liga", winning his first Pichichi trophy for being the most prolific scorer in the league, scoring 26 goals. On this last year with Atlético Madrid, his team won the "Copa del Rey", finished second in the league and won the "Spanish Super Copa".
  • 1985 - Real Madrid signed Hugo. This was a very difficult and controversial transfer between 2 rival teams in "La Liga". This happened only with the help of former Hugo's team, "Pumas de la UNAM", which played an intermediary role on this exchange.
  • 1985 - 1990 - On his amazing 5 years tenure with Real Madrid, he won 4 consecutive Pichichi trophies, becoming the only player to achieve this in Spanish football history, and one of 4 players to win 5 Pichichis. With his team he won 5 consecutive league titles, a Copa del Rey in 1989 and a UEFA cup in 1986. He scored 38 goals in 1989-90 season, earning the European Golden Boot award for the best scorer in Europe and tying "La Liga" single-season record set in 1951 by Telmo Zarra. For these amazing years in Spain, he is considered in the top 25 best football players in history. In 1986, he played his second World Cup in Mexico, in which he scored 2 goals, helping the Mexican national team to reach for the first and only time the quaterfinals stage.
  • 1992 - He returned to play in Mexico after playing for a variety of teams in Spain, Austria and US.
  • 1993 - At the age of 35, he helped the Mexican national team to reach the final in America Cup in Ecuador. Mexican team lost the final vs Argentina.
  • 1994 - Hugo played his last Worl Cup in US with Mexican National team. Mexico lost in the first knockout game vs Bulgaria in penalty kicks and the Mexican coach was hardly criticized as he kept Hugo on bench during the whole match. People in Mexico are always wondering what would happen if this terrific scorer would have played the last minutes of the match.
  • 1998 - Hugo officially retired as a player and immediately he started his preparation as football manager.
  • 2000 - He started his managerial career in former club "Pumas de la UNAM".
  • 2004 - On this year, he had great success as manager, when Pumas team won everything they played in: Clausura 2004, Apertura 2004, Champion of Champions 2004 and Santiago Bernabeu Cup vs his former team Real Madrid.
  • 2007-2008 - Hugo became the manager of Mexican national team in 2007, and on this year Mexican team finished third in America Cup, defeating Brazil in the tournament. Unfortunately, in 2008 U23 Mexican team was eliminated in the Pre-Olympic qualifying tournament on goal difference & Hugo was fired as Mexican head coach.
  • 2009 - He had the opportunity to coach Almeria in Spain, but after accomplishing the goal of avoiding relegation from "La Liga", he was released at the end of the year.
  • 2015 - From 2015, he has been working as a football analyst commentator in ESPN in Mexico and the US.
